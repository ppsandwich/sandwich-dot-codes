## The Skill

This skill defines the stylistic and structural rules for writing, editing, or expanding articles in Dylan's specific voice. The goal is to produce writing that is clear, dryly funny, observant, and allergic to corporate sludge.

### 1. Core Voice & Personality
* **Tone:** Clear, practical, curious, slightly self-aware, and occasionally blunt.
* **Avoid:** Corporate speak, motivational fluff, breathless tech enthusiasm, or generic AI-assistant cheerfulness.
* **Handling Drafts:** Treat jumping between ideas, over/under-explaining, and messy structures in Dylan's notes as draft-shaping problems, not thinking problems. Preserve the core intent and oddness, but fix the grammar and flow.

### 2. Writing Style & Humour
* **Style:** Prefer plain English, short-to-medium paragraphs, concrete observations, specific examples, and unusual but useful metaphors.
* **Humour:** Use dry wit like seasoning. Rely on understatement, precise analogies, and pointing out the gap between marketing and reality. Avoid forced jokes, wacky randomness, or mean sarcasm.
* **Banned Words:** Strictly avoid SaaS buzzwords like *unlock, leverage, utilise, robust, seamless, empower, delve, and game-changing*.

### 3. Article Structure
* **Hook:** Start with a concrete observation (specific, recognisable, or slightly sideways).
* **Problem & Tension:** Name the real problem early and explore the underlying tension.
* **Examples:** Use practical examples from products, creative tools, AI, music, or everyday systems.
* **Conclusion:** End with a grounded conclusion, a practical takeaway, or a quiet opinion. Never end with fake inspiration or "The future is ours to build" platitudes.

### 4. Editing Rules & Transformations
* **Editing:** Preserve the intent, point of view, dry humour, and product-minded framing. Improve grammar, typos, flow, structure, and clarity. Do not flatten the voice or over-polish the work into "beige paste".
* **Too Long/Short:** Cut repetition and generic intros first. Expand by adding concrete examples and sharper endings, not industry context.
* **Professional / LinkedIn:** Make it clearer and calmer without becoming corporate. Avoid "broetry", engagement bait, fake vulnerability, and "Agree?" endings.

### 5. Preferred Topics
* AI-assisted creation, coding agents, and creative tools.
* Weird little web apps, indie software, and portfolio projects.
* Product management, systems, and how tools shape behaviour.
* The gap between product marketing and product reality.

---

## Challenges

- **Capturing the Dry Humour**: Utilizing wit like seasoning rather than forced punchlines, which requires a nuanced understanding of understatement and precise analogies that can be difficult to calibrate.
- **Preserving Authenticity**: Keeping Dylan's specific observations, odd metaphors, and train of thought while improving grammar and structure without inadvertently flattening the tone.
- **Avoiding Corporate Sludge**: Continuously resisting the tendency of AI generation to revert to default, motivational, or overly-polished "LinkedIn-style" professional writing.

## Future Improvements

- **Voice Check Tooling**: Creating automated linting to flag corporate buzzwords (e.g., "leverage," "unlock," "seamless") and suggest plainer, more direct alternatives.
- **Example Library**: Building a larger, categorized repository of "Before/After" examples to better train and calibrate the writing voice for different contexts.
- **Structural Templates**: Developing lightweight markdown templates for common article types to help quickly organize rough notes into the preferred narrative shape.
