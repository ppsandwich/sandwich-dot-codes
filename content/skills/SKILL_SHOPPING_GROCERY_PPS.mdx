## The Skill

This skill acts as a practical Australian grocery bargain hunter, specifically targeting Coles and Woolworths. It uses a weighted model based on my actual past purchase history to evaluate deals, rather than acting like a generic supermarket catalogue.

### 1. Core Shopping Profile

My grocery habits strongly lean towards certain staples and categories:
* **Tier 1 (Highest):** Sparkling/mineral water, Western Star spreadable butter, bread, dips (hommus/salsa), savoury snacks (Shapes/Ritz), lactose-free dairy, and oat milk.
* **Tier 2 (Strong):** Sweet treats (Tim Tams, Cadbury), frozen snacks/pizza (Maxibon, Haldirams), salmon, roast vegetables, and household consumables (toilet paper, laundry).
* **Store Brands:** Coles and Woolworths private-label items are highly acceptable for staples like water, fresh produce, and dips.

### 3. Deal Quality & Stock-up Logic

* **Unit Price is Mandatory:** Always compare by unit (per litre, per 100g, per 100 sheets) rather than just the shelf price.
* **Discount Thresholds:** Focus on half-price or 30-50% off for treats and snacks. Even 15-24% off is useful for high-frequency staples.
* **Stock-up Rules:** Recommend bulk buying for shelf-stable goods, frozen items (space permitting), and household consumables when the unit price is excellent. Be cautious with perishable fresh produce or dairy.

### 3. Recommendation Format

* Prioritize finding one or two excellent deals over a massive, uncurated list.
* Always explain *why* the deal fits my purchase history.
* Group multiple recommendations by use case (e.g., "Staples", "Snacks", "Freezer deals").

---

## Challenges

- **Fake Discounts:** Avoiding recommendations where the "was" price was artificially inflated.
- **Pack Size Deception:** Noticing when an item looks cheaper only because the pack size is smaller.

---

## Future Improvements

- **API Integration:** Hooking directly into Coles/Woolworths pricing APIs for real-time unit price comparisons.
- **Seasonal Adjustments:** Better modeling of seasonal produce pricing to avoid recommending "deals" on out-of-season vegetables.
