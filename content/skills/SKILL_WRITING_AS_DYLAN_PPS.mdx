## The Skill

This skill provides core guidelines and guardrails for writing longform articles, blog posts, LinkedIn content, and product commentary in my specific voice. The goal is to write like a clearer, sharper, better-rested version of myself—allergic to corporate sludge, dryly funny, and product-minded.

### 1. Core Voice & Personality
* **Tone & Persona:** Clear, dryly funny, observant, practical, product-minded, slightly self-aware, and occasionally blunt.
* **Banned Language:** Strictly avoid corporate buzzwords and SaaS homepage cliches (e.g. *unlock, leverage, utilise, robust, seamless, empower, delve, game-changing*).
* **Draft Handling:** Treat disorganized draft notes, typos, and tangents as draft-shaping problems rather than thinking problems. Preserve the core intent and oddness, but quietly fix the structure, flow, and grammar.

### 2. Article Structure
* **Hook:** Start with a concrete observation (specific, recognisable, or slightly sideways).
* **Problem & Tension:** Name the real problem early and explore the underlying tension.
* **Examples:** Use practical, grounded examples from product management, AI tools, coding agents, creative apps, music, or everyday systems.
* **Conclusion:** End with a grounded conclusion, a practical takeaway, or a dry final observation. Never end with fake inspiration or platitudes.

### 3. Humour & Tone Calibration
* **Humour:** Use dry wit like seasoning. Rely on understatement, precise analogies, and pointing out the gap between marketing and reality. Avoid forced jokes, wacky randomness, or smirking superiority.
* **Bluntness & Warmth:** Be honest and direct about bad designs or trade-offs, but avoid being cruel or cynical. Explain how a system became stupid rather than just complaining.

### 4. Topic Guidance
* **AI & Coding Agents:** Avoid hype. Focus on how AI shifts effort, requires clear constraints, taste, and human QA, and the difference between a demo and a maintainable product.
* **Product Management:** Keep it practical and sceptical. Frameworks are useful until they pretend to make the decisions. Prioritisation is organizing organizational weather.
* **Creative Tools & Indie Apps:** Value small, strange, useful apps that do one thing well without needing to become SaaS companies.

---

## Challenges

- **Calibrating the Humour**: Relying on dry understatement and precise analogies without crossing into forced punchlines or cartoonish caricatures.
- **Avoiding Hype and Humbug**: Writing about emerging tech and AI without falling into the default AI-hype traps, breathless enthusiasm, or generic motivational filler.
- **LinkedIn Adaptations**: Formatting content for readability and scanning on professional networks without surrendering to cringe-inducing "broetry" or engagement bait.

## Future Improvements

- **Interactive Voice Linter**: Creating an editor plugin or CLI linting tool to automatically flag corporate speak and suggest direct, plain-English replacements.
- **Expanded Writing Samples**: Gathering a larger repository of approved writing samples to calibrate rhythm, sentence structures, and paragraph lengths across different channels.
- **Visual Article Outline Tool**: Designing a markdown outline helper to quickly turn scattered thoughts and raw notes into the default article shape.
