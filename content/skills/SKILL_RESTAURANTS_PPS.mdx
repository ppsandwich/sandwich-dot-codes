## The Skill

This skill uses my fast-food and casual dining rankings to model my dining preferences. It helps identify independent restaurants, pubs, and takeaway spots that focus on bold flavour, casual comfort, and practical weeknight convenience.

### 1. Core Proximity Rules
* **The 15-Minute Limit:** For ordinary meals, prioritise spots within a 15-minute drive from eastern Melbourne, Victoria. Convenience and speed often beat a marginal increase in quality.
* **Special Occasions:** Travel radius can expand if meeting friends, celebrating, or seeking highly specific or hard-to-find cuisines.

### 2. Flavour Profile & Preferences
* **Char & Grill:** Strong preference for flame-grilled chicken, charcoal chicken, peri-peri, Portuguese grill, or Lebanese charcoal meats.
* **Considered Casuals:** Favour high-quality burgers (fresh, structured, and saucy), Mexican-ish comfort food (GYG/burritos with salsa and hot sauce), and loaded carbs (pizza with generous toppings, loaded jacket potatoes).
* **Fuss-Free Dining:** Favour pub parmas, souvlakis, Korean fried chicken, and rich curries. Avoid places with pretentious plating, tiny portions, or menus written in "creative jargon."
* **Truffle Ban:** Do not recommend any dishes or venues that feature truffle, truffle oil, or truffle salt.

---

## Challenges

- **Finding Independent Counterparts**: Translating S/A-tier chain preferences (like Nando's, Grill'd, or Crust) into high-quality, local independent spots around Melbourne's outer eastern suburbs.
- **Accurate Proximity Calculations**: Factoring in real-time traffic, parking difficulty, and pickup readiness to keep total travel and collection time under the 15-minute comfort threshold.
- **Keeping Review Data Up-to-Date**: Dynamically filtering out venues that have suffered recent drops in quality, portion sizes, or seasoning consistency despite high historical averages.
