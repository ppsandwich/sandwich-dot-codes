## The Skill

This skill defines my core cooking constraints, kitchen equipment, strong ingredient dislikes, and preferred flavour families. When suggesting or writing recipes, the goal is comfort, intense flavour, and weeknight speed.

### 1. Cooking Constraints & Timing
* **Timing:** Default to meals taking 60 minutes or less (ideally 25–45 minutes). Avoid long braises, slow cooks, overnight marinades, or multi-hour roasts.
* **Equipment:** Assume standard stove, oven, microwave, and barbecue. Do not assume specialty gear (no air fryer, slow cooker, pressure cooker, sous vide, stand mixer, or food processor).
* **Flavour Hooks:** Every recipe must have a bold flavour hook—a rich glaze, gravy, pan sauce, spice rub, cheesy crust, or tangy herb yogurt.

### 2. Taste Profile & Favourites
* **Proteins:** Heavy preference for chicken (schnitzels, tray bakes, curries), beef (steaks with pan sauces, Bolognese, nachos), pork/sausages (crispy pork tacos, sausage risotto), lamb (Greek-style lamb, fast tagines), and fish (salmon with tahini, crispy fish with pea hommus).
* **Carbs:** Meals should feel substantial, anchored by rice (pilaf, biryani), pasta/gnocchi, potatoes (mash, gratins), or tortillas (tacos, nachos).
* **Absolute Dislike:** Never suggest truffle, truffle oil, or truffle-infused ingredients.

### 3. Flavour Families
* **Rich Savoury Comfort:** Bolognese, ragu, stroganoff, risottos, and cheese-heavy bakes.
* **Bright & Tangy:** Balancing richness with lemon, sumac, garlic butter, gremolata, tahini, or herbed yogurt.
* **Warm Aromatics:** Korma, butter chicken, satay, harissa, peri peri, and mild curries.

---

## Challenges

- **Shortcutting Slow-Cook Flavours**: Recreating the deep, complex flavours of slow-cooked meat (like lamb shanks or ragu) within a strict weeknight-friendly 45-60 minute stove or oven window.
- **Adapting Air Fryer Recipes**: Converting popular air-fryer-optimized recipes to achieve similar crispiness using a standard oven tray bake, pan-fry, or barbecue setup.
- **Balancing Sweetness**: Ensuring sweet-savoury glazes (like sticky orange chicken or apricot chicken) have enough salt, acid, and savoury structure to prevent them from tasting cloying.
